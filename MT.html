Intro to Info Infrastructure

Information infrastructure: communications networks and associated software that support interaction among people, organizations and things.

Keys

  • Telecommunications: long-distance communication, transmission of information from one point to another. / between or among points specified by the user, of information of the user’s choosing,
  • Broadcasting: Transmission of information from one point to multiple points, not specified by users.
  • Telecommunications service: Offered for a fee directly to public
  • Telecommunications carriers: Providers having their own network infrastructures, not aggregators.
  • Telecommunications Equipment (TE): Equipment employed by carriers. (eg. Base station)
  • Customer premises equipment (CPE): Equipment employed by users. (eg. Phone)
  • Analog acoustic energy -> Analog/Digital electrical signal
  • Signal: Crest, trough, phase.
  • Voice spectrum: 300-3000Hz
  • Channel bandwidth, circuit bandwidth, guard band
  • Central office switching

Signal transmission

Modulation & Demodulation

  • Shift baseband high frequency required by antenna

  • Resist interference

  • Multiplexing: Multiple signals on one channel

  • FM & AM modulation

    • FM: Modulate frequency of carrier signal by baseband signal
    • AM: Modulate amplitude of carrier signal by baseband signal

  • Noise: unwanted, detectable signal that co-exists with the intended signal on the circuit.

    • Artificial: Interference
    • Natural: natural electromagnetic interference (universe radiation)
    • Internal interference
    • Signal-to-Noise Ratio (S/N): Quantify the quality of signal transmission, higher is better

  • Directions of transmission

    • Simplex: One way (eg. TV, radio)
    • Half-Duplex: Two ways, once at a time, save the uses of bandwidth (eg. walkie talkie)
    • Full-Duplex

  • Fixed telephone system: PBX & Centrex

    • PBX: In-line calling, CPE
    • Centrex: Out-sourced, TE

Transmission media

Twisted pair wire, Coaxial Cable, Optical Fiber, Hybrid Fiber/Coax (HFC), Radio, Microwave, Satellite

  • Twisted Pair Wire: ADSL & VDSL, availability drops dramatically when distance increase

  • Optical fiber: need to convert electrical signal to light beams

    • Hybrid Fiber/Coax (HFC): Fiber transmitters connect to community, but connect by coax to the house
    • FTTH is increasing

  • Mobile cellular communications

  • Microwave communications: Transmitters must see each other

Telecommunications network

  • Terms:

    • Channel: for signal transmission
    • Circuit: physical connection
    • Path/Route

  • Circuits

    • Star: devices connect to a switcher
    • Ring: devices connected like a ring
    • Meshed: devices are individually connected to each others

Types of networks

  • LAN, MAN, WAN & GAN

  • Packet-switched & Circuit-switched

    • Circuit switching: circuit is reserved for users when connection established, circuit is wholly occupied by a single connection, used for voice calling
    • Packet switching: data are divided into packages, circuit can be used by multiple connection simultaneously

  • Connection-oriented & Connectionless Routing

    • Connection-oriented Routing: virtual circuit is built to make sure packets are sent at one time
    • Connectionless Routing: Packets are sent independently

  • Intranet (server + firewall), extranet & firewall

  • Virtual Private Network (VPN): Function as private network without physical circuit.

Standards

  • Ensure compatibility & inter-operability

  • Standards make by International Telecommunications Union (ITU), Telecommunication Standardization Bureau (ITU-T) & ISO

  • National Regulator: FCC, OFCA

  • Inter-operability

    • Homogeneous: all products from the same manufacturer

    • Heterogeneous: bridging one system to another with software

    • Open architecture: bring all systems to the same standard protocols

      • OSI model: Physical, Data Link, Network Layer, Transport, Session, Presentation, Process or Application

      • Protocol

        • For data: TCP/IP
        • For voice: Signaling System 7 (SS7)

  • Intelligent network & Advanced intelligent network

First industrial revolution

  • Women left villages & worked in mills

  • Increasing need for products used to be made in individual homes

    • Factories & markets appeared

  • Complicated manufacturing processes were divided into diff parts (division of labor), created assembly line

  • Proprietary capitalism limited the scale of factories

  • Popularity of mirrors & photography raise more conscious about individuality

  • People look for experience: conversation is more important than dinner itself

Second industrial revolution

  • Mass consumption as a result of accelerated urbanization called for mass production
  • Railways made mass production feasible
  • In 1908, concept of assembly line is created for mass production of Ford Model T
  • Objective of business was to provide mass consumers affordable products
  • Managerial Capitalism separate ownership & operation management, first deploy by General Motors

Managerial capitalism

  • Establishment of labor union
  • Court call enterprise "person" as works are divided in assembly line

Rapid growing wealth

  • Formation of mid class
  • Education enabled people creating own meanings and opinions
  • Better communication & travel stimulated self-consciousness
  • Extended life span (47.3 in 1900 to 76.4 in 2000) increase willingness to invest individual life
  • More non-physical jobs, women begin to work, single parents become popular
